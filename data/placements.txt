Scope and Career Prospects after BTech in AI International Admissions Research Alumni Career Online Payment Library Student-Faculty Login Career Opportunities and Scope for Growth After BTech in Artificial Intelligence September 20, 2023 18:18 IST | Bennett University SUBMIT YOUR ENQUIRY “I imagine a world in which AI is going to make us work more productively, live longer, and have cleaner energy.”-Fei Fei Li (Professor, Computer Science at Stanford University) Artificial Intelligence has affected almost every sector in recent years. As Andrew Ng (Computer Scientist and Global Leader in AI) said, “It is difficult to think of a major industry that AI has not affected and will not transform. This includes education, healthcare, transportation, communication, retail, e-commerce services, and agriculture. AI is clearly making significant differences in these sectors with its clean pathways.” However, do you know many AI experts, technopreneurs, and academicians believe that the realised potentials of AI are only the tip of the iceberg? And the field is likely to experience major developments and innovations in the coming years and decades. Therefore, if you are considering BTech CSE with AI specialisation, the time is now. It is the perfect time to skill yourself with an AI specialisation from one of the top universities in India for engineering . It will help you to bag the best jobs and as the industry is rising, there will be ample opportunities for career growth and high pay scales. You can also go for MTech in Artificial Intelligence to pursue a career in the research and education field. For the best learning experience with world-class infrastructure, placements, and industry exposure, Bennett University is a good option for UG and PG courses in engineering in the North Zone. National and Global Trends and Scope in AI Scope means opportunities in a particular field. Here we are sharing national and global trends in AI that will provide you with an idea of how to leverage the trends for your career advantage. The following data points are sourced from INDIAai. It is the National AI Portal of India. The portal is a joint initiative of MeitY (Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology), NeGD (National eGovernance Division) and NASSCOM to prepare the nation for an AI future. According to a report published in The Indian Express, AI will generate around 20 million jobs in India by 2025. Automation, Robotics, and the latest AI-based technologies will affect around 0.7 million job roles. It means companies will hire more skilled persons and spend on training or upskilling programs. Skills with high demand in future are Analytical Thinking and Innovation, Active Learning and Learning Strategies, Leadership and Social Influence, Critical Thinking and Analysis, and Technology Design and Programming. The Government of India has launched The National Educational Technology Forum to enhance digital educational infrastructure, digital content and capacity. Atal Innovation Mission will boost the culture of innovation. These initiatives will drive career opportunities for AI specialists and ensure prospective career growth in the coming years. According to the World Economic Forum, AI will help to develop specific job roles that will require specialists in that particular domain like Data Science Specialists, AI Specialists, ML Specialists, Information and Security Analysts, IoT Specialists, and more. On a global level, according to World Economic Forum, automation and robotics will replace around 85 million jobs that will require reskilling and create 97 million new jobs by 2025. Artificial intelligence, content creation and cloud computing will be top professional skills. Indian and global trends suggest people who graduated in BTech CSE with a specialisation in artificial intelligence, machine learning, cloud computing, and data science are placed at vantage point to leverage the advantages of the Industrial Revolution 4.0.
